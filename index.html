About

Structure over noise.

Mathematics & Computer Science student at the University of Trier — with a strong focus on algorithms, stochastics, and data modeling.

Alongside my academic work, I have been building infrastructure and software systems professionally — from hosting administration at RSE Systems to research data management at the Leibniz Institute for Psychology.

My current work centers on a single conviction: software and communication must not be owned by corporations. I am developing the LAMA Open Ecosystem — a framework of tools and principles designed to make decentralized, community-owned software viable, sustainable, and attribution-respecting.

Philosophy & License

The LAMA Open Ecosystem

"Build freely. Credit honestly. Respect the ecosystem."

LAMA is not a project — it is a philosophy codified into a license and a growing set of tools. The LAMA Open Ecosystem License (LOEL v1.0) governs all my public work and rests on three non-negotiable principles.

🔓

Freedom to Build

Use, fork, and build commercially — no restrictions on creativity.

🔍

Radical Transparency

Every contributor must be documented via a mandatory attribution system.

🏛️

Permanent Attribution

Credit to original authors is legally protected and cannot be stripped.

This approach is a direct response to the growing trend of stripping authorship from open-source work. LAMA ensures that openness does not come at the cost of recognition.
